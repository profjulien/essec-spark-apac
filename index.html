Frequently Asked Questions

Does my startup idea need to be AI-related?
No. Your idea can be in any industry. We use AI as the tool to accelerate your validation, not as the topic. Whether you’re exploring fintech, food delivery, or fashion — AI helps you test faster.
Do I need AI experience?
Not at all. We teach every AI workflow from scratch. If you can use a search engine, you can learn this. Curiosity is the only prerequisite.
Does it cost anything?
The programme itself is free. You’ll use free-tier AI tools (we’ll share a recommended list at kickoff). The only real “cost” is your time (~5–7 hours per week).
I don’t have a fully formed idea yet. Can I still apply?
Yes. You need a problem space and a rough direction, but it doesn’t need to be polished. The programme — and AI — helps you sharpen it.
Can I apply with a co-founder?
Yes! Teams of up to 2 are welcome. At least one member must be an ESSEC alumnus/alumna.
What time are the live sessions?
APAC-friendly evenings (e.g. 8pm SGT / 9pm HKT). All sessions are recorded for those who can’t join live.
What happens if I’m not selected for Phase 2?
You still walk away with a clear go/no-go decision backed by real data, a reusable AI validation toolkit, and a network of fellow alumni founders. Many participants will discover their idea needs a pivot or isn’t worth pursuing — that’s a valuable outcome too.
How much time does Phase 2 require?
Phase 2 is more intensive: ~10–15 hours per week for 12 weeks (late Sep–Dec). You’re building a real product and acquiring real users. It’s still designed for people with day jobs, but you’ll need to carve out serious evening/weekend time.
What’s the €500 build grant?
Each Phase 2 team receives €500 from the programme, matched 1-for-1 by the founding team (€1,000 total). This ensures skin in the game while giving you real fuel: hosting, AI tool subscriptions, small ad budgets, domains — whatever accelerates your venture. You report how you used it at Demo Day.
How does the path to Tier 1 programmes work?
We’re building direct relationships with leading APAC accelerators and ecosystem partners. Strong Phase 2 projects with a live MVP and traction will receive warm introductions. The December Demo Day jury includes investors and programme representatives.
